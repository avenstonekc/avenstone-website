What Really Drives Construction Costs

By Avenstone Group · February 2025 · 5 min read

Category: Construction Published: Feb 2025

Every project owner wants two things — quality results and predictable costs. But with so many variables, from material shortages to permitting delays, understanding what really drives project cost is essential.

1. Labor & Skill Availability

Labor shortages remain one of the biggest cost factors in today’s construction market. Skilled trades like electricians, framers, and HVAC specialists are in high demand, which directly impacts both rates and timelines.

2. Material Costs & Supply Chains

Global supply issues can increase costs on lumber, steel, and roofing materials. A transparent contractor will proactively adjust bids as supplier costs shift, rather than surprise you halfway through the project.

“The right planning and communication can save tens of thousands in change orders. Clarity upfront always pays off.”

3. Design Changes Mid-Project

Scope creep — the addition of new features or finishes during construction — is the single largest cause of cost overruns. It’s why we emphasize finalized selections before breaking ground.

  • Finalize your floor plan and finishes early.
  • Request weekly progress updates.
  • Use a written change-order approval system.

4. Permitting, Inspections & Code Upgrades

Municipal review timelines and local code changes can affect everything from your framing to your final inspection. A contractor with experience in your area will anticipate those hurdles before they cost you time and money.

Final Thoughts

Budget accuracy starts with transparency. The best builders don’t hide costs — they explain them. At Avenstone Group, we provide itemized estimates and weekly reports so you always know where your investment stands.
